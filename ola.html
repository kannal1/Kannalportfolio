The story in short

Designed for a half-second glance

A self-directed exploration: what should a scooter's dashboard feel like when the rider can only spare half a second to look at it. Every decision came back to one constraint, legible in motion, in bright sun, with zero learning curve.

The half-second glance wasn't a limitation. It was the entire brief.

The always-on bar

The four things a rider always needs

A persistent strip carries range, ride mode, connectivity, and time, never disappearing, so the essentials are always one glance away no matter what else is on screen.

The home dashboard

Speed and range, everything else recedes

The main view puts speed and range front and center; everything else steps back. High contrast and large type keep it readable in motion and direct sunlight.

Settings

Calm, glanceable configuration

Settings, display, sound, bluetooth, were designed to stay calm and scannable, so adjusting them never pulls attention from the road for longer than a moment.

Passcode & overlays

Security without friction

Passcode entry and modal overlays were designed to be quick and unmistakable, secure without becoming a hurdle at the start of a ride.

Looking back

Constraint as the brief

The half-second glance wasn't a limitation, it was the entire brief, and it made every decision sharper. When the constraint is that tight, every element has to justify its place, and the design gets stronger for it.
